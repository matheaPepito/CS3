Health Care in the Philippines

The Philippine Health Care System is growing with time. While private hospitals focus on enhancing their devices and services to cure specific diseases, public hospitals prevent and educate the public about different health issues. According to International Trade Administration (2022), “The top three causes of death in the Philippines in 2021 were ischemic heart diseases, cerebrovascular diseases, and Covid-19.”. It is basic knowledge to know that private hospitals require a charge. Hence, how do Filipinos suffering from poverty and complicated diseases access safe and quality healthcare? According to the Republic of the Philippines Department of Health (n.d), President Duterte signed the Universal Health Care Bill in 2019, subsequently guaranteeing fair access to on-the-mark and affordable health care goods and services.

Different Health Programs Held in the Philippines

Philippine Red Cross

This program is a community-based volunteering program held in different regions and is the premier humanitarian organization in the country. It has been protecting life since 1947. Providing services such as; blood services, health services, disaster management services, etc.

Adolescent Health and Development Program

This program provides adolescents access to quality, but friendly health facilities, programs, and services. It also aims to provide and guide the Filipino youth. An example of their services is Linggo ng Kabataan which was celebrated on August 12-16, 2021.

Deng-GET Out!

With dengue cases in the Philippines recently increasing, the Department of Health reminds the public to do the 4-S. < UL LI>Search and destroy mosquito-breeding sites, secure Self-protection measures like wearing long pants and long-sleeved shirts and daily use of mosquito repellent, Seek early consultation, and Support fogging/spraying only in hotspot areas where an increase in cases is registered for two consecutive weeks to prevent an impending outbreak.

Chronic-Kidney Disease Prevention and Control

If Chronic-Kidney Diseases are left untreated, CKD can progress to more severe complications that require more immediate interventions. Thus, DOH named June National Kidney Month to educate and bring awareness to the public.

Effects of Health Care Unavailability and Ways to Resolve this

Reality of Philippine Health Care

Causes of Health Care Unavailability

Political, social, and economic inequities are the main reason for the lack of access to healthcare and the poor quality of healthcare services. Inadequate government funding, ignoring patients' concerns owing to societal disparities, and tax smuggling are recent examples that have an impact on the Philippines' healthcare system.

Effects of Health Care Unavailability

The nation has difficulty constructing and sustaining public hospitals due to a lack of funding. As a result, the majority of Filipinos living in the provinces lack access to quality healthcare.

Due to a shortage of medical supplies, their high cost, and a shortage of physicians and nurses as a result of poor pay, patients are not receiving the best care possible.

Ways to Solve the Problems Caused by the Effects

Address the scarcity of doctors. By increasing pay, expanding loan forgiveness programs for medical school, and adding more graduate medical education residency places, we can grow the clinical workforce.

Ensure adequate funding for new facilities and materials.

Reduce government and insurance sector regulatory barriers, such as prior authorization, that interfere with patient treatment and drive up expenses in order to boost the efficiency of the current workforce.
